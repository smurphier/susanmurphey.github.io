Summary:

Latitude vs. Weather


Most of us assume weather is more humid, warm, sunny and calm near the equator...and mostly it is. But only mostly.
In the variations we found surprises, like rain when you just washed your car and forgot an umbrella.
In this case, the wind lashes the coasts, humidity exceeds 100%, and clouds pay no mind to anyone -- the weather version of Muphy's Law.

Using data pulled from OpenWeatherMap's API for 500+ cities chosen by random latitude and longitude, we plotted by latitude the observed temperatures, humidities, cloudinesses and wind speeds using MatPlotLib. Here we provide the visualizations, explanations for each, and the source data for this analysis.
